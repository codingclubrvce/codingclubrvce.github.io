Code Marathon 2018

Event Description

On 18th August 2018, the Coding Club held its 2nd edition of the Coding Marathon. It was an 8 hour long workshop where the students were made to work on brainstorming problems from the field of Data Structues and Algorithms. The sessions covered all topics ranging from basics to complex algorithms and data structures. RVCE Hacks!

Aptitude Round
Coding Round
Previous Next
Event Description

RVCE Hacks! was an intra-collegiate coding competition open to students of all branches and semesters. It is the Coding Club’s first ever event. The preliminary round was held at the IEM Auditorium on 17th January, 2017. The top 40 participants qualified for a second coding round, held on 21st January, 2017 at the ISE department. The event saw wide participation from students across semesters and branches, with 236 participants. Participants were from ISE, CSE, Mechanical, Civil and EC departments. The preliminary round was a logical one, with many puzzles and brain teasers. The coding round was a competition hosted on Hackerrank with participants showcasing their programming prowess trying to make it to the top of the leaderboard. The event was aimed at creating and fostering interest in puzzle solving and coding on campus.

GSoC Head Start

Prof Channa Bankapur
Mr. Ankush Mishra
Previous Next
Event Description

A head start event into GSoC was planned by the Coding Club of RVCE to give the members a good idea about the event, application criteria, work involved and an overall picture of the event. The event had two speakers – Prof. Channa Bankapur and Mr. Ankush Mishra. Prof. Channa Bankapur is currently a professor at PES University. He has worked for technology giants like Google and Adobe, and is also an entrepreneur. He has mentored and guided many students towards GSoC, Google Code Jam and other such events. Mr. Ankush Mishra is a 6th semester student at PESIT- South Campus. He worked with Apache for GSoC ’16.

Talk by Anup Kalbalia

Event Description

Anup Kalbalia is the head of Codechef. He was invited to speak at our college and address the students on the importance of problem solving ability.
