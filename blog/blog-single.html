import numpy as np
from scipy.integrate import odeint
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
# Total population, N.
N = 1000
# Initial number of infected and recovered individuals, I0 and R0.
I0, R0 = 1, 0
# Everyone else, S0, is susceptible to infection initially.
S0 = N - I0 - R0
# Contact rate, beta, and mean recovery rate, gamma, (in 1/days).
beta, gamma = 0.2, 1./10
# A grid of time points (in days)
t = np.linspace(0, 160, 160)
# The SIR model differential equations.
def deriv(y, t, N, beta, gamma):
S, I, R = y
dSdt = -beta * S * I / N
dIdt = beta * S * I / N - gamma * I
dRdt = gamma * I
return dSdt, dIdt, dRdt
# Initial conditions vector
y0 = S0, I0, R0
# Integrate the SIR equations over the time grid, t.
ret = odeint(deriv, y0, t, args=(N, beta, gamma))
S, I, R = ret.T
# Plot the data on three separate curves for S(t), I(t) and R(t)
fig = plt.figure(facecolor='w')
ax = fig.add_subplot(111, axis_bgcolor='#dddddd', axisbelow=True)
ax.plot(t, S/1000, 'b', alpha=0.5, lw=2, label='Susceptible')
ax.plot(t, I/1000, 'r', alpha=0.5, lw=2, label='Infected')
ax.plot(t, R/1000, 'g', alpha=0.5, lw=2, label='Recovered with immunity')
ax.set_xlabel('Time /days')
ax.set_ylabel('Number (1000s)')
ax.set_ylim(0,1.2)
ax.yaxis.set_tick_params(length=0)
ax.xaxis.set_tick_params(length=0)
ax.grid(b=True, which='major', c='w', lw=2, ls='-')
legend = ax.legend()
legend.get_frame().set_alpha(0.5)
for spine in ('top', 'right', 'bottom', 'left'):
ax.spines[spine].set_visible(False)
plt.show()

The future of IOT

IOT July 25, 2018

Picture this,you come home from work in the evening drenched in rain.You reach home to find that the water is heated ,your evening coffee has just been brewed and the T.V. is switched on to your favorite evening show.That would be a great welcome wouldn't it? This scenario seemed like a dream only a couple years ago.But today you can do this and much more using IoT. IoT or The Internet of Things can link any and all of your electronic and digital devices allowing you to control your appliances at the touch of a button. But what more can we expect out of IOT in the near future.

Smart Clothing

Forget about smart watches and bands.By 2020 we will be welcoming smart wearables .Smart wearables are clothes with sensors for tracking your activities.What better way for any device to interface with our body than clothes? This will also have countless advantages in medicine as these wearables could potentially predict a heart attack. Companies like Google,Underarmour,Clothing+ are already making this a reality.

Smart Cities

A smart city is the one that uses data and technology to better serve the needs of the public.In these kind of cities the entire city will be interconnected. The infrastructure and development of the city will improve many-fold. The amount of power saved on streetlights,home lights,thermostats and other electronic devices will be massive. Smart cities will help achieve a better,faster and cleaner world.

Agriculture

Smart farming will allow farmers to know about their yield, infestation of pests ,soil nutrition,moisture levels and more which will allow them to take countermeasures accordingly. It will allow farmers to predict which crop will give them maximum yield and profit based off of data from soil quality ,water quality,market trends etc. It will also allow farmers to predict which crop will give them maximum yield depending on data received from their location ,weather predictions,soil quality,water quality and other such factors.

Artificial Intelligence

With all of your appliances connected and communicating with each other,machines will be able to learn your habits and perform accordingly to suit you. For example if your office begins at 9 and the journey to the office takes an hour ,hot water will be ready by 7:30 and coffee will be ready by 8. If you are running low on any product your phone will order it online for you. This will reduce the "human error" and saves money and time. Soon this will become so integrated into our lives it will be hard to imagine a time when we did not have the benefits of IoT.

Virtual Reality

IoT and virtual reality are a perfect fit for each other. The application of this combination is countless. It can be used in gaming ,architecture,planning and development of cities,medicine etc. You know how in Sci-fi movies people just tap in the air to view cool 3-D holograms? That will be possible very soon using this golden match. The simplest example is Telepresence the technology allowing you to be present in a place you are not actually in. Simply put its the 3-D version of a video call.

Android P is the next big step

Android July 23, 2018

Google has announced Android P, the next major version of its mobile operating system.

The updates provided in a blog post by google mostly cover updates and tools for developers to begin building apps for the new OS. The company has stated it will continue to update the beta and release new builds often, with more news promised in May from Google I/O.

New features

Display features

Google has added Display Cutout support to Android P, meaning the iPhone X-like notches and Android lookalikes are here to stay. Using the new tools, developers can build apps that automatically work around any cutouts, without having to worry about important information or navigation features being hidden by the cutout

Video And Image features

Updates to video playback and image compression are also coming to Android P. Google is adding support for HDR VP9 Profile 2 and HEIF for developers to integrate into their apps. The same goes for a new Multi-Camera API that's for devices with dual rear-facing cameras or dual front-facing cameras.

Message Notifications

One of the more notable features touted by Google is new messaging notifications. The rich notifications improve upon Android's current notifications by adding more context and potentially suggested replies.

Security

Naturally, security and privacy are also a big part of Android P.
